A bit about me and how I got into coding

I am currently completing a Software Developer Skills Bootcamp at The Developer Academy as part of the April 2025 cohort. However, I am still relatively new to coding after learning the basics of web development at home for a few months prior to starting the course. I have a degree in Linguistics and after a brief career in teaching English as a second language, I worked as an Operations Specialist for a medical and security assistance company, helping people all over the world and saving lives for over a decade.

Entering midlife, moving to Yorkshire and leaving my job in London in the space of a fortnight made me pause and think about what I would really like to do for a living going forward. My curiosity and passion for continuous learning, problem-solving and creating solutions that have real-world impact led me to explore the software development path and training opportunities. A career in tech, which seemed impossible at first, suddenly wasn't so impossible anymore. Soon I was hooked on Codewars, reading discussions on Stack Overflow and revising long-forgotten bits of the maths curriculum.

Solving problems and puzzles is great fun but what I enjoy the most is the creative process where I can use these bits of code to make something useful. To see what I have been working on recently, you can check out my projects here or visit my GitHub.
